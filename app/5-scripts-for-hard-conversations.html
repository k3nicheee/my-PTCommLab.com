5 Scripts for Hard Conversations

PT Communication Lab — Free Resource
1. Patient hasn't done their exercises
"That happens. Was there something specific that got in the way this week?"
Alternative: "Let us look at the exercise plan together. If it is too much or too hard to fit in, I want to know. We can adjust."
2. Patient in pain and afraid to move
"I hear you. Fear of making it worse is completely understandable. Let us find a version that does NOT make it worse."
Alternative: "What is the smallest, safest movement you feel willing to try today? Even a tiny one."
3. Patient asks 'How long will this take?'
"Most people with your condition start feeling noticeable improvement in 4-6 weeks, but full recovery takes longer. I cannot promise a date, but I can promise we will track progress weekly."
Alternative: "Instead of 'how long,' let us ask 'what needs to happen for you to feel done?' Let us list those milestones together."
4. Patient is frustrated with slow progress
"I hear your frustration. You are working hard. Let me show you the progress you might be missing."
Alternative: "Progress often looks like stairs, not a ramp. Flat periods, then jumps. You might be on a flat part right now. That is normal. The jump is coming."
5. Patient wants more exercises (you disagree)
"Research shows that when people have too many exercises, they actually do less. I want you doing fewer things really well rather than many things sporadically."
Alternative: "Here is the deal: do these four consistently for two weeks. Then we talk about adding more."
