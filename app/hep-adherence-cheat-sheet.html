HEP Adherence Cheat Sheet

PT Communication Lab — Free Resource
📋 The Evidence in One Sentence
"Fewer exercises, done consistently, beat many exercises done sporadically."
📊 HEP Prescription Starting Point

Number of exercises

2-4 (max)

Frequency

3-5 days/week

Sets

1-2

Reps

8-12

🔼 When to Progress
  • Patient completes prescribed frequency for 2 weeks consistently
  • Pain with exercise is less than 3/10 and settles quickly
  • Patient reports exercises feel "too easy"
🔽 When to Regress
  • Pain with exercise greater than 4/10 that persists after session
  • Patient consistently misses sessions (plan is too hard)
  • Poor form despite cueing
🗣️ What to Say Instead of "Did you do your exercises?"
"Let's focus on just two key movements this week. I want these to feel easy to fit into your day. Which two feel most doable for you?"
"What got in the way this week?"
📚 Research Reminder

Moelands et al. (2024): Higher prescribed frequency and dosage of home exercises significantly lowers patient adherence.

Kearney et al. (2024): In-person supervised exercise had 4.2x higher odds of adherence compared to app-based exercise.

💡 The One Question That Changes Everything
"On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you that you can do this at home?"

If less than 7, ask why. Solve that why.
