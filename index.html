I’m running late for a patient visit because of a vehicle accident. The family may be upset, and I’m not sure when traffic will clear. Should I call, and what should I say? I want to help the patient and maintain trust without getting scolded.
Generate Patient-Friendly Version
Of course. I understand how stressful this situation is for you. Here’s a practical script: “Hi, this is [Your Name], your physical therapist. I first want to apologize for calling so close to our appointment time. I’m stuck in traffic due to a vehicle accident and will be delayed. I truly care about your recovery. I will update you as soon as I know my exact arrival time. Thank you so much for your patience.” Trust is built through honesty, not perfection. You’re doing the right thing by calling.
